## Executive Summary

VaxBrand's geo-level MMM delivers high predictive confidence across all six territories (Ridge R² range: 0.896–0.958; national average 0.928), providing a robust foundation for reallocation decisions. The single most consequential finding is the **Pacific territory's structural under-investment**: at 16% spend share against 20.4% of national market size, Pacific is the largest addressable gap in the portfolio — compounded by above-average HCP responsiveness (hcp_mult=1.1) and the strongest DTC pull-through profile nationally (dtc_mult=1.2), yet the optimizer recommends a *reduction* driven by poor current channel mix efficiency, signalling that the problem is not budget quantum but channel allocation. The headline reallocation recommendation is a **net shift of approximately $56K/period** — moving budget from Midwest (−$37.6K), Mountain (−$15.0K), Southwest (−$30.5K), and Pacific (−$30.5K) toward Northeast (+$62.1K) and Southeast (+$50.4K) — projected to deliver a **+1.0% national NRx uplift** against a ±30% per-cycle operational corridor constraint. Uncertainty is highest in the **Northeast** (Patient Advocacy Partnerships HDI ±69,297 scripts) and **Pacific** (Samples & Co-pay Coupons HDI ±43,796 scripts; lowest Bayesian R²=0.794), making these the priority candidates for geo holdout lift testing before committing to full reallocation.

## Lift Test & Uncertainty Priorities

### Priority 1: Northeast — Patient Advocacy Partnerships Channel Test
**Rationale:** Patient Advocacy Partnerships is the single largest spend channel in Northeast (39.6%, ~$81.6K/period) yet carries the widest Bayesian HDI in the territory at **±69,297 scripts** — the largest absolute uncertainty of any channel-territory combination in the portfolio. The Ridge model attributes meaningful NRx volume to this channel, but the Bayesian model cannot confirm this with confidence. Given that the optimizer recommends scaling Northeast total spend by +30% (to ~$268K), committing an additional ~$62K to a territory where the lead channel has this level of uncertainty is a material risk. **Recommended test design:** Geo holdout in 2–3 Northeast DMAs (matched on HCP density and historical NRx trajectory), suspending Patient Advocacy Partnerships investment for one full period while maintaining all other channels. Measure NRx delta vs control DMAs to establish true incremental contribution before the next budget cycle.

### Priority 2: Pacific — Full Territory Response Validation
**Rationale:** Pacific presents a compound uncertainty case: the **lowest Bayesian R² in the portfolio (0.794)**, the second-widest HDI (Samples & Co-pay Coupons ±43,796 scripts), and the largest market-share under-investment gap (−4.4pp). The Ridge and Bayesian models show the largest R² divergence of any territory (Ridge 0.910 vs Bayesian 0.794 — a 0.116 gap vs the next-largest gap of 0.133 in Midwest), suggesting the territory's response dynamics may be more complex or non-linear than the current model captures. The Pacific market's structural characteristics — high dtc_mult=1.2, compressed season_str=0.65, and a geographically concentrated HCP base in major metro markets — may require a more granular sub-territory model. **Recommended test design:** Geo holdout across Northern California vs Southern California sub-regions, testing the Medical Congress + Speaker Bureau channel mix recommended by the optimizer against the current Patient Advocacy-led mix. This will simultaneously validate the channel reallocation signal and improve model confidence before any budget increase is considered.

## Cross-Territory Strategic Themes

### Theme 1: The Medical Congress & Speaker Bureau Underinvestment Gap — A National Opportunity
Across all six territories, the optimizer's most consistent signal is a **+150% scale-up in Medical Congress & Symposia and Speaker Bureau Programs** — the two highest-ROI channels in the portfolio (0.728 and 0.630 respectively). Yet in the current national mix, these channels are systematically under-weighted relative to lower-ROI alternatives: Field Rep Visits (ROI 0.532) dominate in Midwest and Southwest, Out-of-Home (ROI 0.210) consumes 19.7% of Midwest budget, and DTC Television (ROI 0.280) leads Mountain despite dtc_mult=0.8. The aggregate incremental investment directed to Congress and Speaker Bureau across the reallocation is ~$152.6K/period — the single largest channel-level shift in the plan. For a vaccine brand, this reflects the fundamental importance of **KOL-driven advocacy and peer-to-peer HCP influence** in driving NRx adoption, particularly in territories with above-average hcp_mult (Northeast 1.2, Pacific 1.1). Brand and medical affairs teams should align on Congress calendar prioritisation and speaker faculty development as a national strategic priority.

### Theme 2: DTC Channel Misalignment — High-Potential Markets Leaving Pull-Through on the Table
Pacific (dtc_mult=1.2) and Southeast (dtc_mult=1.15) are the two territories with the strongest patient-channel responsiveness nationally, yet neither has a DTC-optimised channel mix. Pacific's top channel is Patient Advocacy Partnerships (ROI 0.490) — a hybrid channel that does not fully capture the DTC digital pull-through potential of a dtc_mult=1.2 market. Southeast's DTC advantage is partially captured through Speaker Bureau pull-through but is not complemented by a direct patient digital layer. Conversely, Mountain (dtc_mult=0.8) has DTC Television as its *lead channel* — the inverse of what the territory profile warrants. A **national DTC channel architecture review** is warranted: Pacific and Southeast should receive targeted DTC digital investment (social, search, patient portal) to convert the dtc_mult advantage into incremental NRx, while Mountain's DTC Television budget should be redirected to HCP-facing channels.

### Theme 3: Seasonal Compression Requires Pre-Season Investment Front-Loading in Southern and Pacific Markets
Four of six territories operate below the national seasonal average: Pacific (season_str=0.65), Southeast (0.65), Southwest (0.70), and Northeast (0.90). For a vaccine brand, a compressed flu season means the **effective promotional window for driving NRx is materially shorter** in these markets — HCP detailing, Congress investment, and DTC campaigns must be front-loaded ahead of the vaccination season rather than distributed evenly across the period. The two territories with above-average seasonality — Mountain (1.2) and Midwest (1.1) — should be the primary focus of in-season promotional activity, while Southern and Pacific markets require a **pre-season HCP priming strategy** (early Congress attendance, speaker programme activation, and formulary access confirmation) to maximise pull-through during the compressed window. Field force deployment calendars and speaker bureau scheduling should be reviewed against regional seasonal curves, not a uniform national calendar.

## Caveats & Next Steps

### Model Limitations

**1. Territory Model Independence:** All six Ridge and Bayesian models are estimated independently at the territory level. This means **cross-territory spillover effects are not captured** — for example, a national DTC television campaign or a major Congress presence may generate NRx lift in adjacent territories that is attributed to baseline rather than the promotional channel. The aggregate +1.0% projected uplift should be treated as a conservative floor estimate; true national uplift may be higher if cross-territory synergies exist.

**2. Bayesian vs Ridge R² Divergence:** The consistent gap between Ridge R² (range: 0.896–0.958) and Bayesian R² (range: 0.794–0.869) across all territories suggests the Bayesian models are appropriately regularised but may be under-fitting in territories with complex non-linear response dynamics (particularly Pacific at 0.794). This divergence is a feature, not a bug — it reflects genuine uncertainty — but it means the Bayesian credible intervals should be treated as the **conservative planning scenario** and Ridge point estimates as the **optimistic scenario**.

**3. What a Hierarchical Model Would Add:** A national hierarchical (partial-pooling) MMM would allow territories with sparse data (Mountain, $75K/period) to borrow statistical strength from higher-spend territories, reducing HDI width and improving parameter stability. It would also explicitly model the national-level channel effects (e.g., Medical Congress ROI as a national parameter with territory-level deviations) rather than estimating six independent ROI curves. This is the recommended next modelling evolution for VaxBrand's geo MMM infrastructure.
